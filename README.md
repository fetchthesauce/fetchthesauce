## <img src="https://media.giphy.com/media/hvRJCLFzcasrR4ia7z/giphy.gif" width="28"> &nbsp;About Me

I'm a developer who enjoys building practical solutions that automate workflows, simplify complex tasks, and solve real-world problems. My interests span **DevOps**, **Generative AI**, **Cloud Infrastructure**, **System Administration**, and **Full-Stack Development**, and most of my curiosity eventually turns into a new project on GitHub.

I've worked with technologies ranging from **AWS**, **Docker**, **Jenkins**, and **Nginx** to **FastAPI**, **LangChain**, **CrewAI**, and modern LLM ecosystems, building everything from AI-powered applications and RAG systems to automated deployment pipelines and cloud-native services. I also enjoy the systems side of computing, from Linux administration and server management to troubleshooting the occasional operating system mystery.

I enjoy understanding the trade-offs behind technical decisions. Whether it's infrastructure, hardware, software, or operating systems, I tend to favor solutions that provide meaningful practical benefits, even when they aren't the most popular or visually appealing option.

When I'm away from the terminal, I'm usually refining my Linux setup, experimenting in my homelab, self-hosting services, tinkering with Windows OS, or probably gaming, watching anime, or reading a light novel or manga.

## 📜 How It Started

📄 At 10, wrote my first HTML program and discovered browser Inspect Element, which quickly turned into an obsession with understanding how things worked behind the scenes.

📱 Started breaking and fixing Android phones at 13 through custom ROMs and ROM porting.

🐧 Discovered Linux at 14 and quickly fell down the rabbit hole of open-source software, system administration, and cybersecurity.

🛡️ At 16, began responsibly reporting security vulnerabilities, including an open redirection issue in one of Google's sandbox domains, and later received a Certificate of Appreciation from Intel.

⚙️ Looking back, every rabbit hole somehow led to another one.
